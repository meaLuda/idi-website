Our DID Process

Our Decision Intelligence Design process revolves around a continuous decision flow:

Sense → Frame → Model → Decide → Act → Learn

—that guides practitioners from spotting signals to embedding insight-driven actions and improvements.

Each stage builds on the last:

  • • sensing real-world data,
  • • framing clear questions,
  • • modeling evidence-based options,
  • • deciding with confidence,
  • • acting through purposeful implementation, and
  • • learning from outcomes to restart the cycle stronger.

Mastering this rhythm equips teams to make smarter, faster, and more responsible decisions in any context.

Decision Intelligence Design Fellowships

Transform complexity into actionable solutions through our immersive fellowship programs designed for impact across sectors.

These fellowships immerse fellows in hands-on, high-impact roles across public sector agencies, private sector partners, civil society communities and international development organizations to collaborate with cross-functional teams, implement projects, and measure real-world impact, culminating in strategies that sustainably scale innovations beyond the fellowship. This practical experience ensures long-term sustainability and positions fellows as catalysts for Kenya's innovation ecosystem.

Fellowship Structure

Projects Immersion and Implementation (Months 1-5)

  • • Government & Private Sector Onboarding: Fellows integrate into host agencies and organizations, gaining an in-depth understanding of departmental structures, strategic objectives, and innovation needs.
  • • Project Roadmapping: Working closely with mentors and team leads, participants formulate detailed project plans, identifying key milestones, success metrics, and resource requirements.
  • • Hands-On Execution: Fellows apply the principles of decision intelligence, AI, and systems thinking to real-world challenges, developing and testing solutions within their host organizations.
  • • Iterative Feedback Loops: Fellows present interim findings and prototypes to stakeholders, refining strategies based on user feedback and institutional needs.
  • • Solution Optimization & Scaling: Fellows fine-tune their solutions for broader adoption, ensuring readiness for scale-up across multiple departments or partner organizations.
  • • Impact Measurement & Reporting: Using structured frameworks, fellows evaluate social, economic, and operational outcomes, demonstrating the tangible value added to their host institutions.

Program Summit (Month 6)

  • • Transition & Sustainability Planning: Fellows outline sustainability strategies, policy recommendations, or handover processes to ensure their work continues to thrive beyond the fellowship.
  • • Culmination & Graduation: The internship placements conclude with a recognition ceremony where fellows present their final projects, showcasing breakthroughs and lessons learned to a wider audience of government officials, private sector leaders, and innovation stakeholders.
